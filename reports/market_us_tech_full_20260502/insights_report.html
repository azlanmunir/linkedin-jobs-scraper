LinkedIn jobs market intelligence

AI is real, but the market is still mostly experienced engineering work.

A 7-day scrape across SF Bay Area, New York, Boston, Seattle, and Austin produced 59,506 raw listing snapshots and 7,354 canonical jobs after LinkedIn job-ID dedupe.

7,354 canonical jobs analyzed
7.5% dedicated AI/ML roles
56.1% postings mention AI/ML/LLMs
1.5% entry or intern roles

Executive Read

Dedicated AI/ML roles are meaningful but not dominant: 553 of 7,354 jobs. The stronger signal is horizontal AI diffusion. AI language appears across software, product, data, infrastructure, security, research, and management postings.

The clearest labor-market signal is seniority pressure. Mid, senior, staff, manager, and director roles dominate. Entry-level demand is barely visible in this sample.

Core SWE still leads. SWE is the largest single bucket at 19.1% of canonical jobs.
AI is a layer, not the whole market. AI/ML is 7.5% as a role family, but AI terms appear in 56.1% of postings.
Junior hiring is thin. Entry plus intern roles total 107 postings, only 1.5% of the corpus.

Bottom Line

The original question was whether AI/ML demand is real and what the tech market actually looks like. The answer from this run is: AI/ML is real, but standalone AI jobs are not the whole market. AI has become a broad layer over software, data, product, infrastructure, and security work. The strongest practical signal is that companies want experienced builders, operators, and technical leaders who can ship in AI-tinged production environments.
